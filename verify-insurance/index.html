What happens next

What Happens After You Submit Your Insurance Information?

After you submit your insurance information, our admissions team reviews your benefits and contacts you to explain what we find. We can help you understand coverage for residential treatment, PHP, IOP, and other mental health treatment options when clinically appropriate.

  1. We Review Your Information

    Our admissions team reviews the insurance details you provide and may contact you if anything is missing or unclear.

  2. We Check Your Benefits

    We contact the insurance provider to review benefits, eligibility, possible authorization requirements, and coverage for mental health treatment.

  3. We Explain Your Options

    A team member walks you through what may be covered, what questions still need to be answered, and what level of care may be worth discussing.

  4. You Decide the Next Step

    Verifying insurance does not commit you to treatment. You can use the information to decide whether to move forward, ask more questions, or speak with admissions.

Coverage

What Mental Health Treatment May Insurance Cover?

Many insurance plans cover mental health treatment when care is medically necessary. The exact coverage depends on your benefits, diagnosis, authorization requirements, and the level of care recommended after clinical screening.

Residential Mental Health Treatment

Insurance may cover residential mental health treatment when symptoms require 24/7 structure, psychiatric support, therapy, and a higher level of care than outpatient treatment can provide.

Learn About Residential Treatment

Partial Hospitalization Program

PHP may be covered when a client needs full-day structured treatment without overnight residential care.

Learn About PHP

Intensive Outpatient Program

IOP may be covered when a client needs structured outpatient treatment that is more intensive than weekly therapy.

Learn About IOP

Veterans Mental Health Services

Veterans, active-duty service members, and eligible family members may have coverage options through TRICARE, TriWest, VA Community Care, or commercial insurance.

No commitment

Verifying Insurance Does Not Commit You to Treatment

Many people hesitate to verify insurance because they worry they are signing up for treatment, triggering a charge, or making a final decision. That is not the case.

Insurance verification is an information-gathering step. It helps you understand what your plan may cover so you can make a more informed decision.

A benefits check can help you understand:

  • Whether your plan is active
  • What mental health benefits may be available
  • Whether residential treatment, PHP, or IOP may be covered
  • Whether authorization may be required
  • What out-of-pocket costs may apply
  • Whether in-network or out-of-network benefits may be available
  • What questions to ask before starting treatment

Privacy

Your Insurance Information Is Handled Confidentially

We understand that insurance information is personal. Mental Wellness KS uses your information only to contact you, verify benefits, and help you understand treatment options.

Insurance companies may receive diagnostic, authorization, and medical-necessity information if treatment moves forward. They do not receive detailed therapy notes or private session content as part of a basic benefits check.

We will not:

  • Charge you for submitting the verification form
  • Commit you to treatment without your consent
  • Share your information with family members without proper authorization
  • Pressure you into a level of care that is not clinically appropriate
  • Promise coverage before benefits are reviewed

Veterans and service members

TRICARE, TriWest, and VA Community Care Benefits

Mental Wellness KS is veteran-owned and works with veterans, active-duty service members, eligible family members, and referral sources seeking mental health treatment.

Coverage may be available through TRICARE, TriWest, VA Community Care, or commercial insurance depending on eligibility, referral requirements, authorization, medical necessity, and level of care.

TRICARE

TRICARE may cover medically necessary mental health treatment for eligible active-duty service members, retirees, and family members when requirements are met.

TriWest

TriWest may help eligible veterans access community care when authorized through the appropriate pathway.

VA Community Care

Some veterans may be eligible for care through VA Community Care when the VA cannot provide timely or geographically accessible care that meets clinical needs.

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Insurance Verification

Is insurance verification free?

Yes. Submitting your insurance information for a benefits check does not commit you to treatment and does not require payment for the verification itself.

Does verifying insurance mean I have to start treatment?

No. Insurance verification simply helps you understand what your plan may cover. You decide whether to move forward after speaking with admissions.

How long does insurance verification take?

Timing can vary depending on the insurance provider, the accuracy of the information submitted, and whether additional details are needed. Admissions will contact you as soon as there is information to review.

What information do I need to verify insurance?

Helpful information includes the insurance provider name, member ID, group ID if available, policyholder name, date of birth, and contact information.

Will insurance cover residential mental health treatment?

Many insurance plans cover residential mental health treatment when it is medically necessary. Coverage depends on your plan, diagnosis, authorization requirements, benefits, and level of care.

Will insurance cover PHP or IOP?

Many insurance plans cover PHP or IOP when medically necessary. Coverage depends on your plan, clinical needs, authorization requirements, and the level of care recommended.

Do you accept TRICARE or TriWest?

Mental Wellness KS works with TRICARE and TriWest pathways when eligibility, authorization, referral requirements, and medical necessity criteria are met. Admissions can help review your options.

What if my insurance is out of network?

Some plans include out-of-network benefits that may still help cover treatment. Admissions can review your benefits and explain what options may be available.

Can a family member verify insurance for a loved one?

A family member may be able to help provide insurance information, but privacy and consent rules may apply depending on the situation. Admissions can explain what information is needed.

What if I do not have insurance?

If you do not have insurance, call admissions to discuss available payment options and whether Mental Wellness KS may be the right fit.
