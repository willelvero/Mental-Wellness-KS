A note before you walk through

A private residence on a quiet street, reimagined for treatment.

Mental Wellness KS is not a hospital. It is a single-family home in a residential neighborhood, kept small on purpose so a small, consistent staff can know every client by name.

What follows is the house as it actually is — the bedrooms our clients sleep in, the kitchen they eat in, the room where group meets every morning, the pool where afternoons get quieter. Light, calm, and built for the work.

Six bedrooms·Adults, 18+·Up to twelve clients in residence

01 / 05

The Common Room

A daylit room with a long sectional, a pool table, and the kind of soft, unstructured time that recovery actually depends on. Morning coffee happens here. So does the chess game that runs all week.

02 / 05

The Kitchen

Meals are shared, not catered. The island seats six and is where most of the day's small, quiet conversations begin — between clients, between clinicians, between a client and the staff member who already knows their chart.

03 / 05

The Bedrooms

Six bedrooms, configured for one or two clients each. Real linens, real light, no fluorescent ceilings. Sleep is one of the first things we work on, and the rooms are built to support it.

04 / 05

The Bathrooms

Walk-in showers, warm wood, marble. Small things — but the kind of small things that matter at the end of a long therapy day. Each bathroom is shared by no more than two clients.

05 / 05

The Courtyard

Private, gated, and shaded by mature trees. Group sometimes moves outside. Most evenings end here — twelve people, a pool, the desert mountains turning pink behind the palms.
