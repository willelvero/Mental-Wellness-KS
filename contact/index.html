What happens next

What Happens After You Contact Us?

Reaching out is a first step. It does not lock you into treatment or require you to make a decision on the spot.

  1. We Review Your Message

    Admissions reviews the information you provide and follows up using your preferred contact method whenever possible.

  2. We Talk Through What Is Happening

    You can share as much or as little as you are ready to share. The goal is to understand symptoms, safety, treatment needs, and what questions you have.

  3. We Discuss Level of Care

    Admissions can help explain whether residential treatment, PHP, IOP, emergency care, or another option may be worth considering after clinical screening.

  4. We Help You Understand the Next Step

    If Mental Wellness KS may be appropriate, our team can discuss insurance verification, admissions, travel, family involvement, and timing. If another level of care is needed first, we can help explain that clearly.

When to reach out

You Can Contact Us About More Than Admission

Many people contact us before they are sure treatment is the next step. That is okay. Admissions can help you understand options without pressure.

People commonly reach out to ask about:

  • Whether residential mental health treatment is appropriate
  • Whether PHP or IOP may be a better fit
  • How insurance verification works
  • How to help a loved one who is struggling
  • What to do after hospitalization or crisis stabilization
  • Whether veterans mental health services are available
  • Whether Mental Wellness KS treats a specific condition
  • How family involvement works
  • What to expect before arrival
  • How quickly care may be available

Emergency support

If This Is a Crisis, Use Emergency Support Now

Mental Wellness KS is not an emergency crisis response service. If you or someone you love is in immediate danger, may act on suicidal thoughts, is unable to stay safe, or may harm themselves or someone else, do not wait for a form response.

Call or Text 988

The 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline is available for mental health crisis support, suicidal thoughts, emotional distress, and urgent safety concerns.

Call 911 or Go to the Nearest Emergency Room

Use emergency care if there is immediate danger, a medical emergency, serious injury, intoxication, or risk of harm.

Veterans: Call 988 and Press 1

Veterans, service members, National Guard and Reserve members, and loved ones can call 988 and press 1 for the Veterans Crisis Line.

If the person is medically stable and you are looking for residential treatment, PHP, IOP, or insurance verification, call Mental Wellness KS at (866) 460-4462.

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Contacting Mental Wellness KS

Does submitting the contact form commit me to treatment?

No. Submitting the contact form does not commit you to treatment. It simply allows admissions to follow up, answer questions, and help you understand possible next steps.

How soon will someone contact me?

Response times can vary depending on when the form is submitted and whether any information is missing. If you need to speak with someone now, call (866) 460-4462.

Can I call for a loved one?

Yes. Family members often call to ask about symptoms, safety, levels of care, insurance, and what to do next. Privacy and consent rules may apply depending on the situation.

Can professionals or referral sources contact you?

Yes. Therapists, case managers, hospitals, clinicians, and other professionals may contact Mental Wellness KS to discuss referral options and clinical fit.

What should I have ready before I call?

Helpful information may include the person's current symptoms, treatment history, safety concerns, medications, insurance information, and what type of support you are looking for. You do not need to have everything ready before calling.

Can you verify insurance through the contact form?

The contact form can start the conversation, but the Verify Insurance form is the best place to submit insurance details. Admissions can also help gather information by phone.

What if I am not sure what level of care is needed?

That is one of the main reasons to contact admissions. The team can help explain residential treatment, PHP, IOP, emergency care, and other options based on symptoms and safety.

What if this is an emergency?

If there is immediate danger, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988. Veterans can call 988 and press 1.
