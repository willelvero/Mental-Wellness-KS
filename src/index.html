Levels of Care

Our Programs

Residential Mental Health Treatment

Twenty-four-hour structured care for adults whose symptoms have outpaced outpatient support. Residential treatment may include psychiatric care, therapy, medication support when clinically appropriate, family education, daily structure, and step-down planning.

Learn More

Veteran Mental Health Services

Veteran-owned, trauma-focused mental health services for veterans and active-duty service members struggling with PTSD, trauma, depression, anxiety, moral injury, sleep disruption, and co-occurring concerns when clinically appropriate.

Learn More

Partial Hospitalization Program

PHP is a structured, full-day level of mental health treatment without overnight residential care. It typically includes therapy, group programming, psychiatric support when appropriate, skills practice, and step-down planning for clients who need more support than weekly outpatient care.

Learn More

Intensive Outpatient Program

IOP is a structured outpatient level of care that provides more support than weekly therapy while allowing clients to continue daily responsibilities. It typically includes therapy, group support, coping skills, relapse-prevention planning, and ongoing clinical guidance.

When outpatient therapy is not enough

When Mental Health Symptoms Need More Support

Many people start with weekly therapy, medication appointments, or support from family. For some adults, that is enough. For others, symptoms begin to affect sleep, safety, relationships, work, family life, or the ability to function day to day.

Mental Wellness KS helps adults and families understand whether residential treatment, PHP, IOP, outpatient care, or emergency stabilization may be the right next step. Every inquiry is reviewed carefully so admissions can recommend the safest and most appropriate level of care.

Treatment may be worth considering when:

  • Weekly therapy has not been enough
  • Depression, anxiety, trauma, PTSD, bipolar disorder, OCD, or psychosis-related symptoms are worsening
  • A loved one is struggling and the family is unsure what to do next
  • Sleep, work, school, relationships, or daily functioning are being affected
  • There has been a recent crisis, hospitalization, or step-down need
  • Self-harm or suicidal thoughts are present but emergency care is not currently required
  • Medication changes, mood instability, or psychiatric symptoms need closer support
  • Insurance verification or level-of-care guidance is needed

If someone is in immediate danger, unable to stay safe, or at risk of harming themselves or others, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988.

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Mental Health Treatment

What does Mental Wellness KS treat?

Mental Wellness KS treats adults struggling with depression, anxiety, trauma, PTSD, bipolar disorder, OCD, schizophrenia, psychosis-related concerns, self-harm, suicidal ideation, and co-occurring mental health symptoms when clinically appropriate. Admissions reviews each inquiry to determine whether our level of care is the right fit.

Is Mental Wellness KS a residential mental health treatment center?

Yes. Mental Wellness KS provides residential mental health treatment in Palm Springs, California. Residential care offers 24/7 structure for adults who need more support than outpatient therapy can provide.

How do I know whether I need residential treatment, PHP, or IOP?

The right level of care depends on symptoms, safety, medical stability, treatment history, support at home, and what has or has not worked before. Admissions can help you talk through the situation and determine whether residential treatment, PHP, IOP, outpatient care, or another level of support may be appropriate.

Does insurance cover mental health treatment?

Many commercial insurance plans cover mental health treatment when care is medically necessary. Coverage depends on the plan, diagnosis, benefits, authorization requirements, and level of care. Mental Wellness KS can verify benefits and explain available options before treatment begins.

Do you treat veterans and active-duty service members?

Yes. Mental Wellness KS is veteran-owned and provides veteran mental health services for veterans and active-duty service members when clinically appropriate. Treatment may support PTSD, trauma, depression, anxiety, moral injury, sleep disruption, and co-occurring concerns. TRICARE, TriWest, VA Community Care, and commercial insurance pathways may be reviewed during admissions.

Can families call on behalf of a loved one?

Yes. Families often call when they are worried about a loved one and unsure what level of care is needed. Admissions can help families understand treatment options, safety concerns, insurance verification, and next steps. Privacy and consent rules may apply depending on the situation.

Is Mental Wellness KS an emergency crisis service?

No. Mental Wellness KS is not an emergency crisis response service or locked psychiatric hospital. If someone is in immediate danger, unable to stay safe, experiencing acute psychosis or mania, or at risk of harming themselves or others, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988.

How do I start?

Call (866) 460-4462 or verify insurance online. Admissions can help you discuss symptoms, safety, treatment history, insurance coverage, and whether Mental Wellness KS may be the right fit.
