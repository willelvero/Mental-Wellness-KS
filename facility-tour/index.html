Before you walk through

A Private Residence Reimagined for Mental Health Treatment

  • Six bedrooms
  • Adults 18+
  • Up to twelve clients in residence
  • Residential mental health treatment
  • Palm Springs, California

Mental Wellness KS is a single-family home in a quiet Palm Springs neighborhood, adapted for residential mental health care. The setting is intentionally small so clients are not lost in a large, impersonal program. A consistent team can get to know each client, understand their treatment plan, and support the day-to-day work that happens between therapy sessions.

The house is part of the treatment experience. Clients sleep here, eat here, meet for group here, talk with staff here, and begin rebuilding routine here. The environment is calm, light, and residential because stability often depends on more than the therapy hour. It also depends on sleep, meals, structure, privacy, connection, and the feeling of being somewhere safe enough to do difficult work.

Why the setting matters

A Calm Environment Can Support the Work of Treatment

Residential mental health treatment is not only about removing someone from daily stress. It is about creating a setting where therapy, psychiatric support, group work, medication support when appropriate, family education, and daily structure can begin to hold.

For many clients, the environment itself becomes part of the reset. A smaller setting can reduce overwhelm. A consistent routine can support stability. A private home can feel less intimidating than a hospital. Shared spaces can help clients practice connection without being forced into a large institutional environment.

Explore Residential Treatment

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions About the Facility

Is Mental Wellness KS a hospital?

No. Mental Wellness KS is not a hospital or locked psychiatric unit. It is a residential mental health treatment setting in Palm Springs for adults who are clinically appropriate after screening.

How many clients live at the facility?

Mental Wellness KS is intentionally small, with six bedrooms and up to twelve clients in residence.

Are the bedrooms private?

Bedrooms may be configured for one or two clients depending on availability, clinical needs, and admissions coordination.

Who is the facility for?

Mental Wellness KS serves adults 18+ who need residential mental health treatment for conditions such as depression, anxiety, trauma, PTSD, bipolar disorder, OCD, psychosis-related concerns, self-harm, suicidal ideation after stabilization, and other mental health concerns when clinically appropriate.

Can families visit the facility?

Family involvement and visiting details depend on the treatment plan, clinical appropriateness, consent, and program guidelines. Admissions can explain what to expect.

What if someone needs emergency psychiatric care?

Mental Wellness KS is not an emergency crisis response service. If someone is in immediate danger, unable to stay safe, experiencing acute psychosis or mania, or at risk of harming themselves or others, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988.
